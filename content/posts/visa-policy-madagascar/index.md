### Visa-Free Access

Madagascar does not offer visa-free access to any nationalities.

## e-Visa and ETA Options

Currently, Madagascar does not offer an e-Visa or Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) option for travelers. All eligible nationalities must either obtain a visa on arrival or adhere to the visa requirements specific to their nationality.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

When planning a trip to Madagascar, travelers should be aware of the entry requirements that apply to their nationality. For those eligible for a visa on arrival, it is advisable to have the following documents ready:

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-madagascar/body_5.jpg)
*Photo by [Chen EdisoN](https://www.pexels.com/@chen-edison-2155188199) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*


1. A valid passport with at least six months of validity remaining.
2. Proof of onward travel, such as a return ticket or itinerary.
3. Sufficient funds to cover the duration of the stay.

Travelers should also consider the following practical tips:

- It is recommended to check the latest travel advisories and health requirements, including any vaccinations that may be necessary before arrival.
- Currency exchange services are available at the airport and in major cities, but it is wise to have some local currency on hand for immediate expenses.
- Language can be a barrier, as Malagasy and French are the official languages. Familiarizing oneself with basic phrases can enhance communication.

By understanding the visa policy and entry requirements, travelers can better prepare for their journey to Madagascar.
