India permits visa-free entry for citizens of Bhutan and Nepal. These travelers can enter the country without a visa and stay for an indefinite period, making it convenient for them to visit India.

Additionally, the Maldives enjoys a special provision, allowing its citizens to enter India without a visa for up to 90 days. This arrangement facilitates easier travel for Maldivian nationals.

For travelers from Japan, South Korea, and the [United Arab Emirates](https://visafree.techpawz.com/posts/united-arab-emirates-visa-free/), India offers a visa on arrival option. This allows these nationals to obtain a visa upon arrival in India, streamlining the entry process for short visits.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

When planning a trip to India, it is crucial to be aware of the entry requirements based on your nationality. Travelers should ensure they have the necessary documentation, such as a valid passport and, if applicable, a visa. 

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-india/body_5.jpg)
*Photo by [Mahmut Yılmaz](https://www.pexels.com/@mahmutyilmaz) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*


For those eligible for an e-visa, it is advisable to apply online well in advance of travel to avoid any last-minute complications. The e-visa process is generally straightforward, but travelers should ensure they meet all requirements outlined during the application.

For nationals who can enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival, it is still important to carry valid identification and any other documents that may be required by immigration officials upon entry.

In summary, understanding India's visa policy is essential for a smooth travel experience. By knowing whether a visa is required, what type of visa to apply for, and ensuring all necessary documents are in order, travelers can enjoy their time in India without unnecessary complications.
