## e-Visa and ETA Options

Currently, Costa Rica does not offer an e-Visa or Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) system for travelers. All visitors must adhere to the visa requirements based on their nationality as outlined in the visa policy.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

When traveling to Costa Rica, it is important to be aware of the entry requirements in addition to the visa policy. All travelers must present a valid passport with at least six months of validity remaining from the date of entry. It is also advisable to have a return ticket or proof of onward travel, as this may be requested by immigration authorities upon arrival.

Travelers should ensure they have adequate travel insurance that covers health care and emergencies while in Costa Rica. Additionally, it is recommended to check for any specific health requirements, such as vaccinations, especially for travelers coming from countries with certain health risks.

In summary, understanding the visa policy and entry requirements for Costa Rica is crucial for a successful trip. By knowing whether a visa is needed and preparing the necessary documentation, travelers can enjoy their time in this beautiful country without any complications.
