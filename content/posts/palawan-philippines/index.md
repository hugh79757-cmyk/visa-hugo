## Why Visit Palawan?

Palawan, often dubbed as the “last frontier” of the Philippines, offers a breathtaking blend of natural beauty, rich biodiversity, and vibrant culture. With its stunning limestone cliffs, pristine beaches, and crystal-clear waters, it’s no wonder that this tropical paradise has become a favorite among travelers. The region is home to the famous Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the enchanting El Nido and Coron, both renowned for their picturesque lagoons and coral reefs. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or simply looking to relax on the beach, Palawan caters to all types of travelers.

Beyond its scenic landscapes, Palawan boasts a unique cultural tapestry woven from indigenous tribes and colonial influences. The local cuisine, steeped in flavors and traditions, is a celebration of the region’s rich history. With friendly locals and a laid-back atmosphere, Palawan offers an authentic experience that feels both welcoming and unspoiled. It’s a slice of paradise that feels worlds away from the hustle and bustle of daily life.

## Best Time to Visit Palawan

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_2.jpg)


The best time to visit Palawan is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. During these months, you can expect sunny skies, lower humidity, and minimal rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities like island hopping and snorkeling. December to February is peak season, attracting the largest crowds, especially around the holidays. Prices for accommodations and tours may also rise during this time, so it’s wise to book in advance.

March to May sees a gradual increase in temperatures, with April often being the hottest month. While this is still a great time to visit, be prepared for the warmer weather and slightly larger crowds. The rainy season, from June to October, brings heavy rainfall and occasional typhoons, which can disrupt travel plans. However, if you don’t mind the occasional downpour, you can find lower prices and fewer tourists during this time, especially in locations like Puerto Princesa.

## Where to Stay in Palawan

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_3.jpg)


Choosing the right neighborhood can enhance your Palawan experience. Here are some recommendations across different budget tiers:

- Budget: For travelers on a shoestring budget, staying in the Puerto Princesa area is a solid choice. This city serves as a hub for most flights and offers a range of affordable hostels and guesthouses. The proximity to the airport and local attractions makes it convenient for exploring.
- Mid-Range: El Nido is perfect for those seeking a balance between comfort and cost. Here, you’ll find charming boutique hotels and cozy inns that provide stunning views of the bay. The vibrant atmosphere, along with easy access to island tours, makes El Nido a popular choice among mid-range travelers.
- Luxury: If you’re looking to splurge, consider the resorts in Coron. This area is known for its luxurious accommodations, often featuring private beaches and exclusive amenities. The breathtaking views of the surrounding islands provide an unforgettable backdrop for relaxation and indulgence.
- Remote Getaways: For those seeking a more secluded experience, consider staying on one of the smaller islands, like Malcapuya or Banana Island. These locations offer a more intimate setting with fewer tourists, perfect for a romantic getaway or a peaceful retreat.

## Top Things to Do in Palawan

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_4.jpg)


- Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage site features one of the longest underground rivers in the world, winding through a stunning limestone karst landscape. Guided boat tours provide a glimpse into this natural wonder and its unique ecosystem.
- Island Hopping in El Nido: A visit to El Nido is incomplete without exploring its stunning islands. Tours A and C are particularly popular, taking you to hidden lagoons, secret beaches, and snorkeling spots teeming with marine life.
- Snorkeling in Coron: Known for its crystal-clear waters and vibrant coral reefs, Coron is a snorkeler’s paradise. Dive into the waters surrounding Kayangan Lake and Barracuda Lake for an unforgettable underwater experience.
- Visit the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park: If you’re a diving enthusiast, Tubbataha is a must-visit. This UNESCO World Heritage site is renowned for its rich marine biodiversity and is accessible via liveaboard diving trips.
- Explore the Beaches of Port Barton: Less commercialized than El Nido, Port Barton offers pristine beaches, friendly locals, and a relaxed vibe. Spend your days lounging on the sand or taking a boat tour to nearby islands.
- Discover the Cultural Heritage of Puerto Princesa: Visit the Palawan Heritage Center to learn about the island’s rich history and diverse cultures. The center showcases artifacts and exhibits that highlight the indigenous tribes of Palawan.
- Hiking in Mount Iglit: For the adventurous, hiking Mount Iglit in the northern part of Palawan offers stunning views and a chance to see the unique flora and fauna of the area. It’s an exhilarating way to experience the natural beauty of the island.
- Kayaking in the Mangroves: Rent a kayak and paddle through the mangroves in Puerto Princesa. This serene experience allows you to connect with nature and observe various bird species and marine life in their natural habitat.
- Visit the Floating Village of Busuanga: Experience the local culture by visiting the floating village in Busuanga. Here, you can interact with the locals, learn about their way of life, and enjoy fresh seafood.
- Relax at Sabang Beach: Located near the underground river, Sabang Beach is a tranquil spot with soft sands and clear waters. It’s the perfect place to unwind after a day of adventure.

## Food and Dining Guide

![palawan-philippines](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/palawan-philippines/body_5.jpg)


Palawan’s culinary scene is a delightful mix of flavors and influences. The local cuisine often features fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and indigenous ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes during your stay:

- Tamilok (Woodworm): A unique delicacy found in Palawan, this dish is often served raw, marinated in vinegar, and enjoyed by adventurous eaters. It’s a must-try for those looking to experience local flavors.
- Kinilaw: Similar to ceviche, kinilaw is made with fresh fish marinated in vinegar, lime, and spices. This refreshing dish is perfect for a hot day by the beach.
- Adobo: A classic Filipino dish, adobo is made by marinating meat (often chicken or pork) in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices. Each region has its twist, so be sure to try the local version.
- Lechon: Known as the star of Filipino feasts, lechon is a whole roasted pig with crispy skin and tender meat. It’s a must-try for any meat lover.
- Fresh Seafood: Being an island destination, Palawan offers an abundance of fresh seafood. Whether you dine at a local eatery or a beachside restaurant, indulging in grilled fish or shrimp is a must.
