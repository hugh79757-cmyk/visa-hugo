## Cambodia Visa Policy Overview

Cambodia has established A Practical visa policy that governs the entry of foreign nationals into the country. With a total of 198 nationalities recognized, the policy outlines specific requirements for visa-free access, visa-on-arrival options, and countries that require a visa prior to arrival. Understanding these regulations is essential for travelers planning to visit Cambodia, ensuring a smooth entry process.

## Visa-Free and Visa-on-Arrival Access

![visa-policy-cambodia](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-cambodia/body_2.jpg)


Cambodia offers various categories of entry for foreign nationals, including visa-free access and visa-on-arrival options.

### Visa-Free Access

Certain nationalities can enter Cambodia without a visa. This category is further divided based on the duration of stay allowed:

- Visa-Free for 30 Days: Citizens from Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Singapore, and Vietnam can stay in Cambodia for up to 30 days without a visa.
- Visa-Free for 14 Days: Nationals from Brunei and Thailand are permitted to enter Cambodia for a period of 14 days without requiring a visa.
- Visa-Free: The Philippines is the only country whose citizens can enter Cambodia without a visa for an unspecified duration.

## e-Visa and ETA Options

![visa-policy-cambodia](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-cambodia/body_3.jpg)


Cambodia also provides an electronic visa (e-Visa) option for travelers from eligible countries. This service allows applicants to apply for a visa online before their arrival. The e-Visa is typically valid for a stay of up to 30 days and can be used for tourism purposes.

Travelers should check the official Cambodian e-Visa website for specific eligibility criteria and application procedures. This option can streamline the entry process and reduce waiting times at the airport.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

![visa-policy-cambodia](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-cambodia/body_5.jpg)


When planning a trip to Cambodia, it is essential to be aware of the entry requirements. Travelers should ensure that their passport is valid for at least six months beyond their intended date of departure from Cambodia. Additionally, it is advisable to have a return ticket and proof of sufficient funds for the duration of the stay.

For those obtaining a visa on arrival, it is recommended to carry a passport-sized photograph and the visa fee in cash, as credit card facilities may not be available at all entry points.

Travelers should also stay informed about any health and safety regulations, including vaccination requirements, that may be in place at the time of their visit.

In summary, understanding the visa policy of Cambodia is crucial for ensuring a smooth entry into the country. By knowing whether a visa is required, the duration of stay permitted, and the necessary documentation, travelers can better prepare for their journey.
