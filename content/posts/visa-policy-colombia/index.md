## Countries That Require a Visa

Travelers from countries not included in the visa-free or e-visa categories will need to obtain a visa before entering Colombia. This requirement applies to those whose nationalities are not listed in the previous sections. It is essential for these travelers to consult the nearest Colombian embassy or consulate to understand the specific visa application process and requirements.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

When planning a trip to Colombia, travelers should be aware of the general entry requirements. All visitors, regardless of their visa status, must have a valid passport with at least six months of validity remaining from the date of entry. Additionally, travelers may be asked to provide proof of onward travel, such as a return ticket or a ticket to another destination.

It is advisable for travelers to familiarize themselves with the customs regulations and any restrictions on items that can be brought into the country. Health insurance is also recommended, as it can provide coverage for medical emergencies during the stay.

In summary, Colombia's visa policy is designed to facilitate travel for a wide range of nationalities, offering various options from visa-free access to e-visas. Understanding these requirements is crucial for a smooth entry into the country.
