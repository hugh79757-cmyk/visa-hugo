## Countries That Require a Visa

Travelers from countries not listed under the visa-free or e-visa categories are required to obtain a visa before entering Guinea. This includes nationals from countries not mentioned in the previous sections, which totals 21 nationalities. It is advisable for these travelers to check with the nearest Guinean embassy or consulate for specific visa application procedures and requirements.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

When planning a trip to Guinea, it is essential to be aware of the entry requirements. Travelers should ensure that their passport is valid for at least six months beyond their intended stay in Guinea. Additionally, it is recommended to have a return ticket and proof of accommodation during their visit.

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-guinea/body_5.jpg)
*Photo by [Vika Glitter](https://www.pexels.com/@vika-glitter-392079) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*


For those applying for an e-visa, it is advisable to complete the application well in advance of travel to allow for processing time. Travelers should also familiarize themselves with any health and safety regulations, including vaccination requirements, which may be in place due to regional health concerns.

In summary, understanding Guinea's visa policy is crucial for ensuring a smooth entry into the country. By knowing whether a visa is required, and the options available, travelers can prepare accordingly for their visit to Guinea.
