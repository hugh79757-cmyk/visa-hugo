## Countries That Require a Visa

Travelers from countries not included in the visa-free or visa-on-arrival categories must obtain a visa prior to entering Indonesia. This requirement applies to nationals from the remaining countries not listed in the previous sections. It is essential for these travelers to check the specific visa application procedures and requirements based on their nationality before planning their trip.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

When traveling to Indonesia, it is important to be aware of the entry requirements. All travelers must possess a valid passport with at least six months of validity remaining from the date of entry. Additionally, travelers should have a return ticket or proof of onward travel. 

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-indonesia/body_5.jpg)
*Photo by [Ega Morgan](https://www.pexels.com/@egamorganstanza) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*


For those applying for an e-visa, it is advisable to complete the application well in advance of the intended travel date to allow for processing time. Travelers should also ensure that they have the necessary documentation, including any required photographs and payment information.

Upon arrival in Indonesia, travelers should be prepared for potential health checks or additional entry requirements, particularly in light of ongoing global health considerations. It is recommended to stay updated on any travel advisories or regulations that may affect entry into the country.

In summary, understanding Indonesia's visa policy is crucial for a smooth entry into the country. By knowing whether a visa is required, and the options available, travelers can better prepare for their journey to Indonesia.
