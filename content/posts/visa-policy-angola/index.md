## Angola Visa Policy Overview

Angola’s visa policy outlines the requirements for foreign nationals wishing to enter the country. With a total of 198 nationalities, the policy categorizes countries into various groups based on their visa requirements. These groups include those that can enter visa-free, those eligible for an e-visa, those who can obtain a visa on arrival, and those that require a visa prior to arrival.

## e-Visa and ETA Options

![visa-policy-angola](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-angola/body_3.jpg)


Angola provides an e-visa option for citizens of three specific countries: Cuba, Samoa, and Venezuela. This electronic visa allows travelers from these nations to apply for their visa online, streamlining the entry process into Angola.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

![visa-policy-angola](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-angola/body_5.jpg)


Travelers planning to visit Angola should be aware of the entry requirements based on their nationality. It is crucial to check whether a visa is needed prior to travel. For those eligible for visa-free entry or e-visas, it is advisable to ensure that all documentation is in order, including a valid passport with at least six months of validity remaining.

Additionally, travelers should be prepared for health and safety regulations, which may include vaccinations or health declarations. It is recommended to stay informed about any travel advisories or updates regarding entry requirements, as these can change based on various factors.

Understanding the visa policy and requirements will facilitate a smoother entry into Angola, allowing travelers to focus on their plans while visiting the country.
