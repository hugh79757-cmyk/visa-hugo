## e-Visa and ETA Options

Currently, Lithuania does not offer an e-Visa or Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) for travelers. Those who require a visa must apply through the appropriate consulate or embassy prior to their travel.

## Entry Requirements and Practical Tips

When planning a trip to Lithuania, it is important to consider the entry requirements. For those who do not require a visa, ensure that your passport is valid for at least three months beyond your planned departure date from the Schengen Area. Additionally, travelers should have sufficient funds for their stay and a return ticket.

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/visa-policy-lithuania/body_5.jpg)
*Photo by [www.kaboompics.com](https://www.pexels.com/@karola-g) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*


For those needing a visa, it is advisable to apply well in advance of your travel dates. The application process may require supporting documents such as proof of accommodation, travel insurance, and financial means. It is essential to check with the nearest Lithuanian consulate or embassy for specific requirements and processing times.

Overall, understanding the visa policy and requirements for Lithuania will help ensure a smooth entry into the country.
