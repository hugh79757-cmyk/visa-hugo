Antigua and Barbuda Visa Policy Overview
#

Photo by Duren Williams on Pexels

Antigua and Barbuda is an attractive destination for travelers from various parts of the world. The visa policy of Antigua and Barbuda allows for a diverse range of entry options depending on the nationality of the traveler. With a total of 198 nationalities, the visa requirements vary significantly, with some countries enjoying visa-free access, while others may need to apply for an e-visa or a traditional visa before traveling.

Visa-Free and Visa-on-Arrival Access
#

Photo by Kishawnie James on Pexels

Travelers from certain countries can enter Antigua and Barbuda without a visa. These countries are categorized based on the duration of their visa-free access.

#

Citizens from the following 97 countries can stay in Antigua and Barbuda for up to 180 days without a visa: Albania, Andorra, Argentina, Armenia, Australia, Austria, Azerbaijan, Bahamas, Barbados, Belarus, Belgium, Belize, Botswana, Brazil, Brunei, Bulgaria, Canada, Chile, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Fiji, Finland, France, Georgia, Germany, Greece, Guyana, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Jamaica, Japan, Kazakhstan, Kenya, Kiribati, Kyrgyzstan, Latvia, Lesotho, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Madagascar, Malawi, Malaysia, Maldives, Malta, Marshall Islands, Mauritius, Mexico, Moldova, Monaco, Namibia, Nauru, Netherlands, New Zealand, North Macedonia, Norway, Panama, Papua New Guinea, Peru, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Samoa, San Marino, Serbia, Seychelles, Singapore, Slovakia, Slovenia, Solomon Islands, South Africa, South Korea, Spain, Suriname, Swaziland, Sweden, Switzerland, Tajikistan, Tanzania, Trinidad and Tobago, Turkey, Turkmenistan, Tuvalu, Uganda, Ukraine, United Kingdom, United States, Uzbekistan, Vanuatu, Vatican, Venezuela, Zambia.

Visa-Free for 90 Days
#

Travelers from the following 5 countries can enter Antigua and Barbuda without a visa for up to 90 days: Bolivia, Ecuador, Kosovo, Russia, United Arab Emirates.

Visa-Free for 30 Days
#

Citizens from these 5 countries can stay in Antigua and Barbuda for up to 30 days without a visa: China, Cuba, Hong Kong, Macao, Taiwan.

Visa-Free Access
#

Additionally, nationals from the following 6 countries can enter Antigua and Barbuda without a visa: Colombia, Dominica, Grenada, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines.

e-Visa and ETA Options
#

Antigua and Barbuda offers an e-visa option for travelers from 85 countries. This electronic visa simplifies the application process, allowing travelers to apply online before their trip. The following countries are eligible for the e-visa:

Afghanistan, Algeria, Angola, Bahrain, Bangladesh, Benin, Bhutan, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cambodia, Cameroon, Cape Verde, Central African Republic, Chad, Comoros, Congo, Costa Rica, DR Congo, Djibouti, Dominican Republic, Egypt, El Salvador, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Gabon, Gambia, Ghana, Guatemala, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Haiti, Honduras, India, Indonesia, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Ivory Coast, Jordan, Kuwait, Laos, Lebanon, Liberia, Libya, Mali, Mauritania, Micronesia, Mongolia, Montenegro, Morocco, Mozambique, Myanmar, Nepal, Nicaragua, Niger, Nigeria, North Korea, Oman, Pakistan, Palau, Palestine, Paraguay, Philippines, Qatar, Rwanda, Sao Tome and Principe, Saudi Arabia, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Somalia, South Sudan, Sri Lanka, Sudan, Syria, Thailand, Timor-Leste, Togo, Tonga, Tunisia, Uruguay, Vietnam, Yemen, Zimbabwe.

Countries That Require a Visa
#

Travelers from countries not listed under the visa-free or e-visa categories will need to obtain a traditional visa before entering Antigua and Barbuda. This requirement applies to various nationalities, and it is essential for travelers to check their specific visa requirements based on their nationality.

Entry Requirements and Practical Tips
#

When traveling to Antigua and Barbuda, it is crucial to ensure that you meet all entry requirements. Aside from possessing a valid visa or e-visa, travelers should also have a passport that is valid for at least six months beyond their planned departure date from Antigua and Barbuda. Additionally, travelers may be asked to provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds for their stay.

It is advisable for travelers to familiarize themselves with the local customs and regulations to ensure a smooth entry process. Checking for any updates on entry requirements before traveling is also recommended, as policies may change.
